Kowloon Attractions

Near Holiday Inn Golden Mile, Tsim Sha Tsui (尖沙咀 / Jiānshāzuǐ)

active

Attractions

Avenue of Stars 星光大道 / Xīnguāng Dàdào

Salisbury Road waterfront | 10 min walk | Free

Waterfront walk on the Tsim Sha Tsui Promenade (尖沙咀海滨花园 / Jiānshāzuǐ Hǎibīn Huāyuán) with statues and handprints of Hong Kong film stars. Faces Victoria Harbour (维多利亚港 / Wéiduōlìyà Gǎng) and the Hong Kong Island skyline.

night viewfreeiconic

Star Ferry 天星小轮 / Tiānxīng Xiǎolún

Tsim Sha Tsui Pier | 8 min walk | HK$5 or less

Short ferry ride across Victoria Harbour (维多利亚港 / Wéiduōlìyà Gǎng) to Central (中环 / Zhōnghuán). The upper deck costs slightly more and has better views. A fast, cheap way to reach Hong Kong Island.

transportcheapviews

Tsim Sha Tsui Clock Tower 尖沙咀钟楼 / Jiānshāzuǐ Zhōnglóu

Near Star Ferry Pier | 10 min walk | Free

Former Kowloon-Canton Railway clock tower. The only surviving structure from the old Kowloon (九龙 / Jiǔlóng) terminus. Good photo spot with the Cultural Centre (香港文化中心 / Xiānggǎng Wénhuà Zhōngxīn) behind it.

historyphotofree

K11 MUSEA K11 MUSEA

18 Salisbury Road | 12 min walk | Free to enter

Art and shopping mall on the Victoria Dockside (维多利亚港海滨 / Wéiduōlìyà Gǎng Hǎibīn) development. Has rooftop views, design shops, and food halls. Family-friendly but upscale.

shoppingartfood

Hong Kong Museum of Art 香港艺术馆 / Xiānggǎng Yìshùguǎn

10 Salisbury Road | 12 min walk | Free general admission

Public museum with Chinese art, Hong Kong art, and special exhibitions. The building has large windows facing Victoria Harbour (维多利亚港 / Wéiduōlìyà Gǎng). Good for a quiet morning.

artfreeair-con

Hong Kong Space Museum 香港太空馆 / Xiānggǎng Tàikōngguǎn

10 Salisbury Road | 10 min walk | Exhibits free, planetarium paid

Recognizable by its domed planetarium. Exhibits cover space flight, the Solar System, and astronomy. The planetarium shows are popular with kids but tickets sell out on weekends.

kidsscienceplanetarium

Temple Street Night Market 庙街夜市 / Miào Jiē Yèshì

Temple Street, Jordan (佐敦 / Zuǒdūn) / Yau Ma Tei (油麻地 / Yóumádì) | 15 min walk | Free

Evening street market known for cheap clothes, souvenirs, gadgets, and open-air food stalls. Also called "Men's Street" (男人街 / Nánrén Jiē). Best after 7 PM.

marketstreet foodevening

Food near the hotel

  • Loong Yuen Cantonese Restaurant (龙苑中菜厅 / Lóng Yuàn Zhōngcàitīng) — inside Holiday Inn Golden Mile. Dim sum (点心 / Diǎnxin) and Cantonese dishes.
  • Bistro on the Mile — hotel buffet, convenient for breakfast.
  • One Dim Sum (一点心 / Yì Diǎn Xīn) — nearby Michelin-rated dim sum, expect a queue.
  • Tim Ho Wan (添好运 / Tiān Hǎo Yùn) — famous cheap dim sum chain.
  • Cha chaan teng (茶餐厅 / Chá Cāntīng) — local cafés on Nathan Road (弥敦道 / Mídūn Dào) for milk tea (奶茶 / Nǎichá), toast, and rice plates.

Practical notes

  • Get an Octopus card (八达通 / Bādátōng) at any MTR station for trains, buses, ferries, and convenience stores.
  • MTR signs and many menus are in English and Chinese.
  • Symphony of Lights (幻彩咏香江 / Huàncǎi Yǒng Xiāngjiāng) starts at 8 PM along the harbour.
  • Temple Street vendors expect some bargaining; most take cash.
  • Air conditioning is strong everywhere — bring a light layer.
Tip: The best skyline photos are from the Avenue of Stars (星光大道 / Xīnguāng Dàdào) at blue hour, just after sunset.
