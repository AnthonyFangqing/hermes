Attraction database

Shenzhen Science and Technology Museum （深圳科学技术馆）

This is the headline. The new venue opened May 1, 2025 in Guangming District. It cost 2.8 billion RMB and covers 130,000 square meters. Zaha Hadid Architects won the design competition. It is one of Shenzhen's "New Era Top Ten Cultural Facilities" and claims to be the world's first science museum themed "Digital Civilization, Innovative Future".

What is actually inside

There are 4 permanent exhibition halls with 950 interactive exhibits. The theme is communication technology. The main thread runs from early information transmission to 5G, AI, and quantum communication.

Hello, World! entrance hall

The lobby is built around a 7-meter smart sphere screen. It acts like a giant pupil and recognizes greetings in 200+ languages. Since the May 2025 opening it has been touched/interacted with about 3,000 times per day. An AI robot named 小科 (Xiao Ke) stands nearby.

Specific zones mentioned in the China Daily source

  • "Smart Shenzhen Industrial Charm" – sandbox city model plus AR telescope. About 2,000 visitors stop here per day.
  • "Communication Three Elements" – quantum information flow visualized on light walls.
  • "Digital Human" – compare yourself in the natural world versus the digital world.
  • Big data waterfall screen – vertical screen showing live-ish data flows.
  • Shenzhen Cloud Tour – a moving walkway with motion interaction so you "fly" over Shenzhen.
  • Gesture and voice interaction zones – body-controlled exhibits.
This place opened recently and is popular. Book exactly at 20:00 six days before your visit. If you miss the window, third-party resale apps may have tickets at markup, but official tickets are the safest.

Huaqiangbei Electronics Market （华强北）

Huaqiang North Road is the densest electronics market in Asia. You can buy anything: drone parts, Raspberry Pi clones, robot kits, retro consoles, cables, LED strips, phone accessories, sensors. Even if you buy nothing, walking it is worth it.

Huaqiangbei Museum

Small museum about how a street market turned into the world's electronics supply chain. Free or cheap. Nice break from shopping. Search 大众点评 for current hours before you go.

What to pair with the bus

Get off at Qianhai Stone Park （前海石公园） for the view of the bay and Hong Kong in the distance. There are bike paths and open grass. Good for a family picnic before or after the bus ride.

Shenzhen Metro Line 20 （深圳地铁20号线）

This is a fully automated, driverless metro line. No driver cab. Sit at the very front and watch the tunnel. It connects to Shenzhen World North convention center and other stations.

BYD SkyShuttle （比亚迪云巴）

BYD's autonomous rubber-tired elevated rail. It looks like a small train on concrete tracks above the street. The Pingshan line is the best known. It loops through BYD's home district.

Drone Shows

Shenzhen runs mass drone shows on holidays and special events. The Paper reported a 2,000-drone show at Happy Coast Coconut Beach during the 2026 May holiday. Shenzhen Talent Park and the "Spring Bamboo Shoot" area also host robot and drone performances.

Meituan Drone Delivery

Meituan operates drone delivery in parks and scenic areas. You order coffee or snacks through the Meituan app and a drone drops it at a pickup kiosk. The Paper reported it is now routine in Shenzhen parks during holidays.

eVTOL and Future Transport Displays

On May 1, 2026, the "Shenzhen Bay Future Transport Port" project was announced. It includes eVTOL (electric vertical take-off and landing craft) demonstrations. This is still early-stage, but if your trip lines up with a demo event, it could be the most futuristic thing on the list.

Exoskeleton Robots and Mecha

During festivals, Shenzhen public spaces sometimes offer exoskeleton try-ons and mecha performances. Kids can suit up and walk around. Check event accounts around Labor Day, Dragon Boat Festival, and National Day.

Shenzhen Talent Park

Park in Nanshan with views of the "Spring Bamboo Shoot" skyscraper. It is a common site for robot and drone demos, plus it has running paths and a good lawn. Combine with a visit to DJI store or Happy Coast.

Museum of Contemporary Art and Urban Planning （当代艺术与城市规划馆）

Near Civic Center in Futian. Free or ticketed exhibitions. Often has shows about Shenzhen's growth from fishing village to tech capital. More architecture/city than pure tech, but useful context.

Shenzhen Red Cube / Longgang Science Center （深圳·红立方）

Big science complex in Longgang. Includes science museum, public art gallery, and planetarium. Less famous than the new Guangming museum but closer to Bantian if you are going to the robot restaurant.

Shenzhen Industrial Exhibition Hall

On the upper floors of the Shenzhen Civic Center. Shows the city's industrial history and future plans. Small, free, and quiet. Good contrast to the flashy new museum.

Design Society / Sea World Culture and Arts Center

Designed by Fumihiko Maki. Has rotating exhibitions about design, tech, and urban life. Located at Sea World in Shekou. Pair with a ferry ride or dinner in Shekou.
