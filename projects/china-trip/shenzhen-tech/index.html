Shenzhen Tech Itinerary

3 days, family of four. Mom is right: Shenzhen is a hardware city.

Day 1: Shenzhen Science and Technology Museum

The new venue opened May 1, 2025 in Guangming District. Theme is "digital civilization, innovative future" with communication technology as the main thread. There are 4 permanent exhibition halls and 950 interactive exhibits. This is the headline tech attraction.

FactDetail
Address8 Guanghui Avenue, Guangming District （光明区光辉大道8号）
MetroLine 6, Guangming Station. Exit and walk.
TicketsRegular 50 RMB; discounted 32 RMB
BookingWeChat mini-program: 深圳科学技术馆. Daily 20:00 releases tickets for 6 days later.
HoursTue-Fri 10:00-17:00; weekends/holidays 09:30-18:30 (May-Oct)
ClosedMondays
RulesNo scooters or balance boards. Kids under 14 need an adult.
Book early. This place opened recently and sells out fast, especially on weekends.

Day 3: Ride an Autonomous Bus

Shenzhen Bus Group runs real autonomous bus routes. This is not a demo track. The first routes operated by a public transit company opened in Qianhai in 2025. Fare is 1 RMB.

FactDetail
RouteB998 and 3 other lines in Qianhai
Fare1 RMB
Vehicle6-meter, 9-seat electric autonomous minibus
SensorsHD cameras, mmWave radar, LiDAR, 360-degree perception
BookingWeChat mini-program: 深巴出行, tap 自动驾驶
StopsMetro stations, malls, residential blocks, CBD, parks
There were also employee-only test minibuses in Nanshan Xili. The Qianhai routes are the ones open to the public.

Other tech you might see

  • Meituan drone delivery in parks and scenic spots.
  • eVTOL displays at 深湾未来交通港.
  • Exoskeleton robot trials in public spaces during festivals.
  • Driverless Shenzhen Metro Line 20 (fully automated).

What I skipped

Tencent HQ, Huawei HQ, DJI Sky City. These are company campuses, not tourist sites. You cannot just walk in without an employee invite. I left them out.
